Did you know?

The Earth's magnetic field, which protects our planet from harmful solar radiation, is not fixed. It undergoes constant changes and even occasional complete reversals. The last magnetic reversal occurred approximately 780,000 years ago, meaning that the North and South magnetic poles effectively switched places. The process of a magnetic field reversal can take thousands of years to complete and has occurred multiple times throughout Earth's history.
